About Commonwealth of Nations (CW)

The Commonwealth of Nations is a voluntary association of 56 member states, many of which are former territories of the British Empire. It promotes democratic values, human rights, economic development, and international cooperation across diverse nations across 6 continents. This unique organization brings together countries with shared historical ties and common values, working together to address global challenges and promote sustainable development.

Committee Topic

Advancing Technology and Education Access Across the Commonwealth Nations

The Commonwealth's work encompasses several key areas:

  • Democracy and good governance
  • Human rights and gender equality
  • Economic development and trade
  • Climate change and environmental protection
  • Education and youth development
  • Health and social development

Through its various programs and initiatives, the Commonwealth addresses pressing global challenges while fostering cooperation among its diverse member states. The organization's work is guided by the Commonwealth Charter, which outlines the core values and principles that unite member countries.

The Commonwealth committee provides delegates with a unique platform to engage with global challenges through the lens of shared values and historical ties. As a diverse group of nations spanning multiple continents, the Commonwealth offers a rich context for addressing contemporary issues while drawing on the collective experience of its member states.

Delegates in the Commonwealth committee will need to consider several important factors:

  • Historical ties and shared values among member states
  • Diverse economic and social contexts of member countries
  • Common challenges and opportunities for cooperation
  • Role of the Commonwealth in global governance
  • Future directions for Commonwealth cooperation

Meet the Dias

Joshua Pham
Chair
A senior student studying at Burnaby North Secondary School, looking forward to retirement. Joshua Pham is pleased to act as Chair for CommonWealth at Spamun. Almost 4 years in the making, Joshua’s Model United Nations career has allowed him to freely express his love for social studies and put his networking skills to the test. He found himself beyond engaging in well-mannered debates, but rather forming core memories and strong friendships with those who were once strangers. It became evident that Model UN will be one of Joshua’s core pillars in high school. When not in formal attire in the committee room, Joshua usually gets himself lost on internet rabbit-holes, annoying his friends with the same dragged-on joke, and struggling to get a job. Nonetheless, Joshua encourages everyone to step out of their comfort zone, but most importantly, valuable experiences in Spamun 2025.
