SPAMUN brings together over 250 participants from the Lower Mainland, serving as an accessible day conference where the values of Model United Nations are held to the highest.

What is SPAMUN?

Southpointe Academy Model United Nations is a student-run high school Model United Nations conference held annually in Delta, British Columbia. Since our inception, SPAMUN has aimed to provide an engaging and accessible introduction to Model United Nations while maintaining a high standard of academic debate. Our conference welcomes delegates from a variety of schools and backgrounds, fostering leadership, diplomacy, and critical thinking in a supportive yet challenging environment.

SPAMUN offers a range of committees designed to reflect current global and contemporary issues. While committee offerings and Secretariat leadership evolve each year, delegates can expect a balance of traditional intergovernmental bodies and innovative specialized committees, as outlined on the SPAMUN website (spamun.ca). Throughout the conference, delegates represent assigned countries, organizations, or individuals, and are tasked with researching policy positions, engaging in debate, and collaborating to draft solutions.

At SPAMUN, delegates develop essential skills in public speaking, negotiation, and problem-solving while navigating complex international dynamics. Through structured debate and collaboration, participants gain a deeper understanding of global affairs and the responsibilities of leadership. The dynamic discussions and immersive simulations at SPAMUN are designed to promote personal growth, confidence, and a lasting appreciation for global citizenship.

Our Mission

SPAMUN’s mission is to empower students to become informed, confident, and engaged global citizens through diplomacy. We strive to create an inclusive and academically rigorous environment where delegates can explore global issues, challenge perspectives, and collaborate on meaningful solutions. SPAMUN develops essential skills in leadership, critical thinking, and public speaking. Our conference encourages active participation, integrity, and respect for diverse viewpoints. We truly believe that all members of SPAMUN will find themselves able to learn, lead and succeed as our future leaders.

Venue

SPAMUN is hosted at Southpointe Academy located at 1900 56 St, Delta. Southpointe has been our gracious host since our inception and has evolved alongside SPAMUN. This year our conference will be taking place on our newly built senior campus which doubles the size of our venue. This slight change to the specific rooms where the conference will take place may cause some confusion however more details are to come.

As well, SPAMUN acknowledges that Southpointe Academy is located on the traditional, ancestral, and unceded territories of the Musqueam, Squamish, and Tsawwassen First Nations. We are grateful to live, learn, and gather on this land, and we commit to reflecting on our responsibilities toward reconciliation and respect for Indigenous peoples.
